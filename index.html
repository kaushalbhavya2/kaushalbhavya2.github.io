Client Consulting
Alinta Energy — Customer Profitability and Bill-Shock Strategy
"Analysed customer lifetime value against acquisition cost to prove Alinta was under-leveraging its highest-value customers."

Problem

Alinta Energy was facing bill-shock complaints and needed a strategy to support customers through financial hardship while managing the transition to renewable energy. The challenge was reactive customer support and no predictive billing mechanism.

Approach

Conducted primary research through customer surveys, applied STEEPLE and SWOT frameworks, built a five-stage customer journey map for the persona "Sakshi," and calculated a CLV versus CAC ratio to assess customer profitability.

Key Insights

73% of surveyed customers reported bill-shock. The CLV to CAC ratio of 18:1 revealed Alinta was generating significant value per customer but failing to protect that relationship. Sakshi's journey showed clear anxiety at the billing stage with no actionable tools.

Recommendations

Three strategic pillars: a real-time consumption awareness tool, a tenure-based loyalty and rewards program, and targeted customer engagement at high-risk billing moments. Reframed the core problem toward predictive billing rather than reactive support.

Marketing Analytics Simulation
ExerciseMinder — 9-Year Campaign ROI and Retention Simulation
"Managed a marketing budget across 9 simulated years using regression analysis and A/B testing to achieve a ROMI of 2.27."

Problem

A fitness app needed to grow its user base while maintaining profitability. The challenge was balancing acquisition versus retention spend as competition increased each year.

Approach

Used regression analysis to identify highest-performing channels each year. Ran A/B tests on messaging and offer types. Tracked customer cohorts and adjusted channel mix based on churn signals.

Key Insights

Email marketing and gamification-based engagement significantly outperformed paid acquisition channels in years five through nine. Retention-focused spend delivered 3x the ROI of new acquisition in the final three years.

Recommendations

Shift budget from broad acquisition to segmented retention campaigns earlier in the product lifecycle. Introduce gamification at the 90-day mark to reduce the highest-risk churn window. Use email as the primary retention channel rather than paid social.

What I Would Do Next

Build a customer segmentation model using RFM scoring to identify high-value cohorts and personalise retention offers. Connect campaign performance to lifetime value by segment, not just channel.

Problem

A health content client needed to grow organic traffic in a competitive niche. The site lacked a structured keyword architecture, meaning content was not aligned to how users actually search across different stages of intent.

Approach

Used Ahrefs and SEMrush to map primary and secondary keywords by search intent. Grouped keywords into six thematic clusters. Designed a pillar-plus-supporting-page content structure to consolidate authority.

Key Insights

Time-based queries carried the highest combined volume at 51,000 searches per month. FAQ-intent keywords were underserved across competitor sites, representing a gap for featured snippet capture.

Recommendations

Publish a primary pillar page targeting "low cholesterol diet" supported by cluster pages for each intent group. Prioritise time-based and FAQ clusters first. Build internal linking from cluster pages to the pillar.

My Process
How I approach marketing problems

Five steps. Every time. No exceptions.

01

Define the problem clearly

Identify the real business challenge. Separate the symptom from the root cause before touching any data.

02

Analyse customer and data

Use data to uncover patterns. Segment before you generalise. Look at who is buying, leaving, and not converting.

03

Generate insights

Translate findings into meaning. Isolate which variables actually move the outcome using regression or cohort analysis.

04

Recommend strategy

Suggest actionable steps ranked by effort versus estimated return. Be explicit about trade-offs.

05

Measure impact

Track outcomes and optimise. Define what success looks like before you start, then read results honestly.
