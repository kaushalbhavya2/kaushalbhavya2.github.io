Alinta Energy — Customer Profitability and Bill-Shock Strategy

A full 19-slide consulting proposal for Alinta Energy covering bill-shock reduction and the energy transition. Delivered by PeakEdge Consulting (RMIT team), the project included primary research, STEEPLE and SWOT analysis, customer journey mapping, and a CLV versus CAC framework that was acknowledged by an Alinta executive as a key performance insight.

An 18:1 CLV to CAC ratio indicates that for every dollar Alinta spends acquiring a customer, it generates $18 in lifetime value. This signals highly efficient acquisition and a strong return on investment — a finding presented directly to the Alinta Energy executive team.

Primary research finding

73% of surveyed customers reported experiencing bill-shock. This was the central trigger for reframing the problem toward predictive billing rather than reactive support.

Customer journey — Sakshi

Built a detailed five-stage journey map (Awareness → Consideration → Decision → Service → Loyalty) for the persona "Sakshi," uncovering anxiety around unexpected bills and a lack of actionable usage tools.

Reframed problem statement

"How might we leverage technology to help customers avoid bill-shock and create tailored solutions that match their energy usage patterns with predictable billing options?"

Strategic recommendations

Proposed a three-pillar deliverable: a consumption-awareness tool, a rewards-based loyalty program, and enhanced customer engagement touchpoints — all tied to a 12-month Gantt plan.
