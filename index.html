Client Consulting
Alinta Energy — Customer Profitability and Bill-Shock Strategy
"Analysed customer lifetime value against acquisition cost to prove Alinta was under-leveraging its highest-value customers."

Problem

Alinta Energy was receiving complaints about unexpected bill spikes and needed a strategy to reduce financial hardship for customers while navigating the transition to renewable energy. The challenge was twofold: reactive customer support and no predictive billing mechanism.

Approach

Conducted primary research through customer surveys and applied STEEPLE and SWOT frameworks to map the external environment. Built a five-stage customer journey map for the persona "Sakshi" and calculated a CLV versus CAC ratio to assess customer profitability.

Key Insights

73% of surveyed customers reported experiencing bill-shock. The CLV to CAC ratio of 18:1 revealed that Alinta was generating significant value per customer but failing to protect that relationship through proactive engagement. Sakshi's journey showed clear anxiety at the billing stage with no actionable tools to manage usage.

Recommendations

Proposed three strategic pillars: a real-time consumption awareness tool, a tenure-based loyalty and rewards program, and targeted customer engagement at high-risk billing moments. Reframed the core problem toward predictive billing rather than reactive support.

Marketing Analytics Simulation
ExerciseMinder — 9-Year Campaign ROI and Retention Simulation
"Managed a $2M+ annual marketing budget across 9 simulated years, using regression analysis and A/B testing to achieve a ROMI of 2.27."

Problem

A fitness app needed to grow its user base while maintaining profitability. The challenge was balancing spend on acquisition versus retention as competition increased each year.

Approach

Used regression analysis to identify the highest-performing channels each year. Ran A/B tests on messaging and offer types. Tracked customer cohorts and adjusted channel mix based on churn signals.

Key Insights

Email marketing and gamification-based engagement significantly outperformed paid acquisition channels in years 5 to 9. Retention-focused spend delivered 3x the ROI of new acquisition spend in the final three years.

Recommendations

Shift budget allocation from broad acquisition to segmented retention campaigns earlier in the product lifecycle. Introduce gamification mechanics at the 90-day mark to reduce the highest-risk churn window. Use email as the primary retention channel rather than paid social.

What I Would Do Next

Build a customer segmentation model using RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) scoring to identify high-value cohorts and personalise retention offers. Connect campaign performance to lifetime value by segment, not just channel.

SEO Strategy
Low Cholesterol Diet — Keyword Research and Content Architecture
"Mapped 60+ keywords across 6 intent-based clusters to design a full content pillar strategy for a health client."

Problem

A health content client needed to grow organic traffic in a competitive niche. The site lacked a structured keyword architecture, meaning content was not aligned to how users actually search across different stages of intent.

Approach

Used Ahrefs and SEMrush to map primary and secondary keywords by search intent. Grouped keywords into six thematic clusters covering core variations, food-focused, time-based, general, and FAQ intent. Designed a pillar-plus-supporting-page content structure.

Key Insights

Time-based queries (e.g. "how to reduce cholesterol in 7 days") carried the highest combined volume at 51,000 searches per month and lowest competition. FAQ-intent keywords were underserved across competitor sites, representing a gap for featured snippet capture.

Recommendations

Publish a primary pillar page targeting "low cholesterol diet" (35,000 per month) supported by cluster pages for each intent group. Prioritise time-based and FAQ clusters first. Build internal linking from cluster pages to the pillar to consolidate authority.

My Process
How I approach marketing problems

Most candidates describe what they did. This is how I think through a problem before doing anything.

01

Define the problem clearly

Separate the symptom from the root cause. A drop in sales is not the problem — it is the signal. The problem is why.

02

Analyse customer and data

Look at who is buying, who is leaving, and who is not converting. Segment before you generalise.

03

Identify key drivers

Use regression, cohort analysis, or keyword data to isolate which variables actually move the outcome.

04

Prioritise high-impact actions

Not everything is worth doing. Rank recommendations by effort versus estimated return and be explicit about trade-offs.

05

Measure and iterate

Define what success looks like before you start. Set a metric, run the test, read the results honestly, and adjust.
